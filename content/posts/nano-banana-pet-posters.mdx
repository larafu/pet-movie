## 2. What is Nano Banana Pro, in simple terms?

You can think of Nano Banana Pro as:

**An image generation and editing model that's particularly good at clean visual design, text, and layout.**

Unlike generic "text-to-image" tools that sometimes struggle with typography and consistency, Nano Banana Pro is designed for:

- High-quality still images (great for posters and thumbnails)
- Accurate, legible text on top of images (titles, taglines, names)
- Reasonable control over layout and style, guided by prompts and reference images

For PetMovie.ai, that makes Nano Banana Pro the perfect candidate to be:

- Our **pet movie poster designer**
- Our **sticker / emoji art director**

## 3. Turning your AI pet movie into a cinematic poster

Let's start with the poster.

### Step 1 — Picking the key moment

After your movie is generated, PetMovie.ai 2.0 will automatically scan it for:

- The most emotionally intense frame
- Or the most visually iconic shot (for example, your dog framed by Christmas lights, or your cat sitting in a sunbeam)

We can also let you manually pick a frame if you prefer a specific moment.

![Example poster from pet movie](https://media.petmovie.ai/petmovie/imgs/dog-funny-family-poster.jpg)

*Example: A cinematic poster generated from a pet movie*

### Step 2 — Feeding Nano Banana Pro a "poster brief"

We then send Nano Banana Pro:

- The selected frame (or few frames) as visual references
- A short poster brief, for example:

> "Create a cinematic movie poster for a heart-warming Christmas hero dog story.
> The title is 'My Little Hero'.
> The pet's name is 'Max'.
> Style: Pixar-style 3D / CG, soft lighting, clear golden title text."

From this, Nano Banana Pro generates multiple poster variations, each:

- Centering your pet as the main character
- Keeping a consistent 3D / CG look that matches the film
- Arranging the title, subtitle and name in a readable, film-like layout

### Step 3 — Output sizes and uses

We plan to offer a few practical outputs:

- **Phone wallpaper size** (vertical)
- **Story / Reel cover size** (9:16)
- **Social post / print-ready size** (4:5 or 3:2)

You'll be able to:

- Share the poster as a standalone piece on social media
- Use it as a cover image for the movie
- Print it as a physical keepsake if you like

In short, **Nano Banana Pro turns your AI pet movie into something that looks like a real film release poster** — but starring your dog or cat.

## 4. From movie frames to 8–12 custom pet emojis

The second part of PetMovie 2.0 is all about stickers and emojis.

We want your pet to be able to **"show up" in your chats**, not just in a video player.

### Step 1 — Finding the best expressions

Our system scans your pet movie for:

- Big emotional beats (joy, surprise, determination, gentle sadness)
- Clear, readable facial expressions and poses

*Capturing expressive moments from the movie for emoji creation*

Think:

- The proud "I saved you" look
- The confused head tilt
- The full-body zoomies moment
- The tiny, sleepy eye squint

From these, we select **8–12 frames** that could work as stickers.

### Step 2 — Stylizing with Nano Banana Pro

We then feed Nano Banana Pro:

- The chosen frames as inputs
- A style brief such as:

> "Turn each frame into a clean, fun sticker.
> Keep the dog clearly recognizable.
> Use a consistent outline and color style.
> Transparent background.
> Ready for chat apps."

Nano Banana Pro outputs a set of **cohesive sticker-style images**, each:

- Centered on your pet
- Cleanly cropped
- Harmonized in style (same line width, shading, outline, etc.)

### Step 3 — Packaging as a sticker / emoji pack

Finally, we package these 8–12 images into a simple format you can import into:

- WhatsApp / Telegram sticker packs
- iMessage sticker apps
- Other platforms that support custom emojis or stickers

The end result: instead of generic dog emojis, you're **reacting in chats with your dog's actual face**, taken from their AI movie.

## 6. Why Nano Banana Pro instead of any random image model?

There are many image models out there. We're betting on Nano Banana Pro for a few reasons:

- It's tuned for **clean text and layout**, which matters a lot for posters
- It plays well with the same kind of prompts and context we already use with Gemini 3 Pro
- It can respect **visual consistency** across a set of images, which is crucial for emoji packs

Most importantly, it fits our long-term vision:

**One consistent visual universe for your pet — across video, posters, and stickers.**
